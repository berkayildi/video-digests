## Key Takeaways

- A jury in Los Angeles found Meta and Google liable for negligently designing products to be addictive, harming young social media users
- This was the first social media addiction trial to reach a verdict, potentially setting precedent for thousands of similar cases
- The case focused on product design features like notifications, endless scrolling, and auto-playing videos rather than content moderation
- Damages awarded were $6 million, but approximately 3,000 similar plaintiffs could lead to roughly $20 billion in total liability
- Legal experts compare this to the landmark tobacco cases that fundamentally changed how an entire industry operates
- Both Meta and Google disagree with the verdict and plan to appeal the decision
- Memory chip stocks surged after Google announced a new algorithm that reduces memory requirements for AI models by sixfold

## TLDR

A Los Angeles jury found Meta and Google liable for designing addictive social media products that harm young users, awarding $6 million in damages in the first trial of its kind. With thousands of similar cases pending, this landmark verdict could result in $20 billion in liability and force fundamental changes to social media business models, similar to what happened with big tobacco.
