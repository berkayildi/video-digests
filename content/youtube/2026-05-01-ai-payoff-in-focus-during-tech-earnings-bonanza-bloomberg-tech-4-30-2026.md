## Key Takeaways

- Amazon, Alphabet, Meta, and Microsoft combined plan to spend $725 billion on AI infrastructure in 2026 — up from earlier $600B estimates — making proof of revenue returns the central question of the earnings cycle.
- Alphabet was the clear earnings winner with Google Cloud posting 60% growth and its AI backlog doubling quarter-over-quarter, validating its vertically integrated strategy of models + chips + cloud.
- Meta fell roughly 10% after earnings for failing to provide a concrete metric showing AI investment is generating tangible returns — investors are growing impatient despite solid ad pricing up 12%.
- Nvidia shares declined despite surging capex announcements because hyperscalers are increasingly building custom in-house silicon and shifting focus toward inference, benefiting Broadcom and Qualcomm.
- Qualcomm surged to its best day since 2019 after revealing it will ship custom AI chips to a major unnamed hyperscaler, with data centre revenue expected in billions by FY2027.
- Anthropic is engaging investors on a fresh funding round that could value the company above $900 billion, potentially surpassing OpenAI. The NSA has been testing Anthropic's model for vulnerability detection.
- Stripe partnered with Google to enable Gemini AI agents to make autonomous purchases via its Agentic Commerce Suite — part of a broader shift where AI agents handle discovery, checkout, and payment inside AI interfaces rather than websites. Google launched the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) as an open standard co-developed with Shopify, Etsy, Wayfair, and Target. Stripe doesn't build the agents — it builds the payment rails, leaving the agent intelligence layer (what to buy, when, why) as an open opportunity for startups. This validates rather than blocks independent agent builders.
- Apple set to report with 13-16% YoY revenue growth, spending far less than peers on AI capex — the hardware-first strategy under incoming CEO John Ternus continues to diverge from the data centre arms race.
- 137 Ventures raised $700M bringing AUM to $15B; early SpaceX investments returned ~100x with IPO expected late June 2026. Fraud warnings emerging in pre-IPO secondary markets where SPV investors may not actually own shares.
- Microsoft trades near yearly low but analysts view weakness as buying opportunity with Azure acceleration expected H2 2026.

## TLDR

Big Tech's April 2026 earnings revealed a $725B AI capex commitment — but only Alphabet proved it's working (Cloud up 60%, AI backlog doubled QoQ). Meta dropped 10% for failing to show ROI. Qualcomm emerged as a custom chip winner, Anthropic may raise at $900B+, and Stripe's Agentic Commerce Suite with Google signals the shift to agent-led commerce where AI handles discovery-to-checkout inside AI interfaces. Stripe builds the payment rails; the agent intelligence layer remains an open opportunity.
