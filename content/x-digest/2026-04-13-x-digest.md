## Key Takeaways

- There's a growing perception gap about AI capabilities, often informed by outdated experiences with free-tier models
- Claude Mythos represents a new tier of powerful AI that's being responsibly previewed with cyber defenders before general release
- The OpenClaw moment marked the first time non-technical audiences experienced cutting-edge agentic AI models
- Despite impressive advances, AGI remains distant with Opus 4.6 and GPT 5.4 scoring only 0.3% on ARC-AGI-3 benchmarks
- AI is currently augmenting rather than replacing human workers in most practical applications
- Real-world AI implementation involves significant technical optimization, as shown by Claude Code's 3x performance improvement
- Enterprise AI deployment requires careful engineering considerations for large-scale codebases

## TLDR

AI experts are highlighting a growing gap between public perception and actual AI capabilities. While new models like Claude Mythos show impressive power, true AGI remains elusive with current models scoring poorly on benchmark tests. Meanwhile, practical AI applications focus on augmenting human work rather than replacement.
